# FESDFA Data Classification

## Purpose

Data classification helps identify the sensitivity of information and determines the appropriate level of protection required.

## Public Data

Information that may be viewed by the public.

Examples:
- Member Name
- Ministry Name
- Position/Title
- City and State
- Ministry Website
- Public Ministry Contact Information
- Membership Status

Security Requirements:
- Maintain accuracy
- Prevent unauthorized modifications

---

## Internal Data

Information intended for FESDFA members and authorized personnel.

Examples:
- Membership ID
- Internal Directory Information
- Membership Renewal Records
- Internal Communications

Security Requirements:
- Access restricted to authorized users
- Logging and monitoring of access

---

## Confidential Data

Information requiring the highest level of protection.

Examples:
- Date of Birth
- Personal Phone Numbers
- Personal Email Addresses
- Administrative Notes
- Approval Records
- Authentication Credentials

Security Requirements:
- Encryption at rest and in transit
- Role-based access control
- Multi-factor authentication
- Regular backups

---

## Security Objective

Ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA) of FESDFA information assets while supporting the organization's mission and operational needs.
